Laser Applications at NorthWorks

At NorthWorks, under the leadership of Dr. D. Warner North, we leverage advanced laser technologies to enhance our expertise in risk analysis, nuclear safety, environmental protection, and consulting. Lasers offer precision, efficiency, and innovation in addressing complex challenges, from monitoring nuclear facilities to assessing environmental risks. Explore our specialized sections below to learn how laser applications intersect with our core areas of focus.

Lasers in Risk Analysis

Lasers enable precise measurements and data collection critical for risk assessment models. Our team uses laser-based tools to enhance the accuracy of risk analysis in high-stakes industries. Learn more about lasers in risk analysis.

Lasers in Nuclear Safety

Laser technologies are vital for monitoring and ensuring safety in nuclear facilities. From detecting material defects to real-time environmental monitoring, lasers support robust safety protocols. Discover lasers in nuclear safety.

Lasers in Environmental Protection

Laser-based sensing and imaging technologies aid in monitoring air and water quality, helping to mitigate environmental risks. Our work integrates lasers to support sustainable practices. Explore lasers in environmental protection.

Lasers in Consulting Projects

NorthWorks applies laser technologies in consulting projects for clients in energy, defense, and environmental sectors, delivering innovative solutions tailored to complex challenges. Read about our laser consulting projects.

Additional Information

Sustainable Fuel Cycle Task Force

Dr. North serves as a member of the Science Panel of this group, which was established following the Obama Administration's actions to curtail further activity on the proposed repository for high-level nuclear waste at Yucca Mountain, Nevada. Information on the Task Force and its Science Panel has been available on its website (sustainablefuelcyclesciencepanel.org, which should be online again soon).

The Science Panel has sent letters, most recently to Secretary of Energy Perry (letter dated November 14, 2017), and previously to him as the nominee for Secretary, (letter dated January 23, 2017) recommending that he make one of his highest priorities the restarting of the Yucca Mountain licensing process.
